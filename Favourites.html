Ollies Favourites

Akira 1988

'Akira' is arguably one of the most influential japanese animated films of all time. The film is directed by Katsuhiro Otomo, who was also the original manga artist. The film played a huge role in the rise and popularity of japanese animation in western society, being release in US theatres in the 90's. This film not only introudced me into anime through its amazing animation, cinematography and memorable score, but also the cyberpunk, dystopian genre which is illustrated throughout.

Back to the Future 2021

For me, 'Back to the Future' is one of those films you watched as a child that you will never forget. Directed by Robert Zemeckis, the film mixes fantastic performances and clever writing to create a hilarious sci-fi family movie that puts a smile on anyones face. The film has now become a pop culture icon and has been referenced in countless other movies and tv shows. I love this film.

2001: A Space Odyssey 2021

'2001' is one of those films that prides itself on visual storytelling. Watching this film was one of the most engaging cinema experiencs I have ever been apart of. Each shot, image, sound effect is perfectly thought out, proving Kubrick really is a master of his craft. Watching this film changed my perspective on how cinema really is an artform, and I think we need more films like this again.

Blade Runner: 2049 2021

Denis Villeneuve is one of my favourite directors of all time, and this film alone proves it. '2049' is the sequel that, in my opinion, surpasses the 1982 original. This film is my number one science fiction film in which perfectly depicts a dystopian neo-noir society built on power. The visuals, cinematography by legendary Roger Deakins, soundtrack by Hans Zimmer and excellent cast all come together to create a story that is intriguing from the beginning to end. I don't use this word lightly, but personally this film is a masterpiece, and was definitly worth paying 5 times to see in cinema!

Prisoners 2021

Also directed by Denis Villeneuve, 'Prisoners' is a slow psychological thriller which follows the search for missing girls after they dissapear one evening. The film is unpredictable and emotionally destructive, purposefully to fully invest the audience into the emotions of all characters involved. Roger Deakins once again returns as being Villeneuve's cinematographer who masterfully captures the loneliness and desperation of the fathers searching for their daughters, and just how far they would go to ensure their daughters return home safetly. 'Prisoners' never loses pace and is a film that leaves the audience guessing and on the edge of their seat.

The Shining 2021

The Shining is a psychological thriller from the mind of Standley Kubrick, filled with tension, thrills and horror throughout. One of the first films that got me into cinema, I will still remember the horror I felt whilst watching this alone, in the dark, with my headphones on. Kubrick and the teams use of editing, cinematography, acting and score all perfectly come together to portray a twisted horror story that has proven itself to be one of the best horror films to date. I have seen this film many times and still love it like the first.
